Common Questions

The things families ask me first.

How much does life insurance actually cost?
Less than most people expect. For a healthy person in their 30s or 40s, meaningful coverage often runs about the price of a couple of streaming subscriptions a month. Your rate depends on age, health, the amount, and the term. I'll show you real numbers before you decide anything.
Do I need a medical exam?
Often, no. Many carriers offer no-exam policies based on a few health questions instead of bloodwork. Coverage amounts can be capped, but approval is usually faster. I'll tell you honestly which path fits you best.
What's the difference between term, whole life, and IUL?
Term covers you for a set period (10, 20, 30 years) at a lower cost — ideal for protecting income or a mortgage. Whole life lasts your whole life and builds cash value. IUL grows with the market's upside, protects against the downside, and pays out tax-free. None is "better" — they solve different problems.
How does mortgage protection with cash back work?
It's a term life policy structured around your mortgage. If something happens to you during the term, your family gets the death benefit and the house stays theirs. If you outlive the term with a Return of Premium rider, you get back 100% of the premiums you paid — a lump-sum check you can apply to your mortgage to pay it off years early.
Are you tied to one carrier?
No. I'm independent, brokered through Family First Life — one of the country's largest agencies and an Integrity company. I shop 20+ A-rated carriers on your behalf. My advice answers to your family, not to a single company's product lineup.
Which states are you licensed in?
11 states: GA, AL, CO, FL, KY, NC, OH, SC, TN, TX, and WA. I work remotely, so we can meet by phone or video wherever you are. If you're outside my footprint, I'll point you to someone trustworthy.
